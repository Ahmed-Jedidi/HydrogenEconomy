The 3 Modes of H2 Oxidation

Same chemical ingredients (H2 + O2), vastly different outcomes based on control and temperature.

MODE 1

Fuel Cell

"Cold Transformation"

Controlled Electrochemical Reaction

Output: Electricity (el)
MODE 2

Combustion Engine

"Hot Transformation"

Controlled Thermal Reaction

Output: Heat (kT)
MODE 3

Explosion

"Radical Chain Reaction"

Uncontrolled H-Explosion

Output: Destruction (kT)

Device Comparison

Electrolyzer

Power → Gas

Input: Water (H2O) + Electricity
H2O
Cathode (-) REDUCTION

Reduction occurs here
→ Hydrogen (H2) is produced

Anode (+) OXIDATION

Oxidation occurs here
→ Oxygen (O2) is produced

Fuel Cell

Gas → Power

Input: Hydrogen (H2) + Oxygen (O2)
H2 + O2
Anode (-) OXIDATION

Oxidation occurs here
→ H2 releases electrons

Cathode (+) REDUCTION

Reduction occurs here
→ O2 accepts electrons

Note: The chemical definitions (Anode=Ox, Cathode=Red) stay the same, but the Polarity (+/-) flips!
